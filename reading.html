My Top 5 Favorite Bits of Media

These things, in my view, live in the venn diagram of technology and "everything else," so to speak; try them out!

Category Title Creator Why I recommend it Link
Book A Song of Ice and Fire George R.R. Martin Swords, magic, and dragons are about as far from "technology" in a traditional sense as you can get. If you're willing to open your mind and look beyond the words, though, you'll find there are lessons to be applied. Plus, the characters and world-building are top tier. Learn more
Book Dune Frank Herbert Politics, ecology, and prophecy woven into a slow-burn epic about power and consequences. Learn more
Show Severance Ben Stiller This is a bit on the nose. It's a surreal corporate sci-fi show that asks what we owe our other selves. It's a good allegory for the kind of work-like balance struggle many of us face. Meticulous production design and great acting. Trailer
Show How I Met Your Mother Carter Bays and Craig Thomas Kind of an odd pick, sure, but I've never seen another show that so gratuitously give out life lessons. Each episode dishes out real-world examples of school, love, and career-relared things. Seeing someone else go through these things helps cement them! I use the 2 o'clock rule every time I'm going to make a decision that could haunt me... Trailer
Movie Source Code Duncan Jones A decorated pilot is part of a top-secret military operation that lets him experience the last few minutes in the life of Sean Fentress, a man who died in a commuter-train explosion. When you're watching this, ask yourself when "working hard" and sticking to doing what you're told can become too much. Trailer
